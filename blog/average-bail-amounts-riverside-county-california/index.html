Average Bail Amounts in Riverside County, California

Published 2026-02-22 • Bright Bail Bonds, Lake Elsinore, CA

Bail amounts in Riverside County, California are set according to the county's bail schedule, which assigns standard amounts to specific criminal charges. Common bail amounts include: DUI first offense ($5,000–$10,000), domestic violence ($50,000), drug possession ($15,000–$25,000), assault and battery ($25,000–$50,000), and felony theft ($50,000+). A judge at the Riverside County Superior Court can adjust these amounts based on the defendant's criminal history, flight risk, and circumstances of the offense. Bright Bail Bonds offers bail bond loans to make any bail amount affordable.

How Riverside County Sets Bail

The Riverside County Superior Court maintains a felony and misdemeanor bail schedule that standardizes bail amounts across the county's court system. This schedule is reviewed and updated annually by the county's superior court judges. When a person is arrested and booked into a Riverside County detention facility — whether the Southwest Detention Center in Murrieta, the Robert Presley Detention Center in Riverside, or another facility — the bail amount is typically set according to this schedule.

Common Bail Amounts in Riverside County

The following are approximate bail ranges for common charges in Riverside County. Actual amounts may vary based on specific circumstances, criminal history, and judicial discretion:

  • DUI / DWI (first offense): $5,000 – $10,000
  • DUI with injury: $50,000 – $100,000
  • Domestic violence (misdemeanor): $25,000 – $50,000
  • Domestic violence (felony): $50,000 – $100,000
  • Drug possession (misdemeanor): $5,000 – $15,000
  • Drug possession for sale: $25,000 – $50,000
  • Petty theft: $5,000 – $10,000
  • Grand theft: $20,000 – $50,000
  • Burglary (residential): $50,000 – $100,000
  • Assault with a deadly weapon: $50,000 – $100,000
  • Robbery: $100,000 – $250,000

Factors That Affect Bail Amounts

While the Riverside County bail schedule provides baseline amounts, several factors can cause a judge to increase or decrease bail at a bail hearing: the severity of the alleged offense, the defendant's prior criminal record, prior failures to appear in court, ties to the Lake Elsinore community (employment, family, residence), whether the defendant poses a flight risk, and whether the defendant is considered a danger to the victim or community.
